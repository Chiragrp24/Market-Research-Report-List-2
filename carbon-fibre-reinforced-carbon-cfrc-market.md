<p><p>Carbon Fibre Reinforced Carbon (CFRC) is a composite material consisting of carbon fibre reinforcement embedded in a carbon matrix. Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) is a process where carbon fibres are grown on a substrate through the decomposition of hydrocarbon gases in a vacuum chamber. Liquid Impregnation Process (LIP) involves impregnating preformed carbon fibres with liquid carbon precursor resin and subsequently heating to form the desired composite. These processes are used in the CFRC market to produce high-performance materials with excellent mechanical properties for various applications.</p></p>
