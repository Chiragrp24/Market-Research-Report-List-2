<p><p>Process oil is a specific type of oil used in various industries to improve the performance and properties of end products. It is a mixture of high-boiling petroleum fractions obtained during the petroleum refining process. Process oil acts as an intermediate or processing agent, enhancing the plasticity, flexibility, and compatibility of materials in industries such as rubber, tire, automotive, textiles, polymer, and lubricants.</p><p>The global process oil market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period. The primary factors driving this growth are the increasing demand from end-use industries and the growing automotive industry. Process oils are extensively used in tire manufacturing, and with the rising demand for automobiles, the demand for process oil is also increasing. Additionally, the increasing construction activities and growing polymer industry are fuelling market growth.</p><p>On the basis of type, the market can be segmented into paraffinic, naphthenic, aromatic, and non-carcinogenic process oils. <p><p>Process oil is a crucial component used in various industries for lubrication, insulation, and cooling purposes. It comes in different types such as naphthenic, paraffinic, aromatic, and others. Naphthenic process oil contains saturated hydrocarbons, suitable for rubber and plastics industries. Paraffinic process oil is made of linear hydrocarbons, making it ideal for industrial rubber goods and electrical cables. Aromatic process oil contains cyclic hydrocarbons, suitable for tires, coatings, and adhesives. Other types of process oil include low aromatic, highly refined, or specialty oils, tailored for specific industrial applications.</p></p>
