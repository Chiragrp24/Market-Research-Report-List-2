<p><p>IGBT (Insulated Gate Bipolar Transistor) and MOSFET (Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor Field-Effect Transistor) are both powerful semiconductor devices used in various applications, especially in power electronics. They both function as power switches, controlling the flow of electrical current, but they differ in terms of structure and operation.</p><p>IGBTs are commonly used in high-power applications due to their ability to handle large currents and high voltages. They combine the best features of MOSFETs and bipolar junction transistors (BJTs), providing low conduction losses and high switching speeds. They are extensively deployed in industries such as automotive, renewable energy, and consumer electronics.</p><p>On the other hand, MOSFETs are known for their low power consumption and excellent switching speeds. They are widely utilized in low-power applications, such as smartphones, laptops, and other portable devices. MOSFETs are highly efficient, enabling longer battery life and reduced power dissipation.</p><p>The IGBT and MOSFET market is expected to experience steady growth in the coming years.
