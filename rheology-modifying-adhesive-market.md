<p><p>The Rheology Modifying Adhesive market includes two major types: organic and inorganic. Organic rheology modifying adhesives are made from natural substances such as gums, resins, and polysaccharides. They provide improved viscosity control and shear-thinning behavior to adhesive formulations. In contrast, inorganic rheology modifying adhesives are synthetic materials like clays and silica compounds. They offer enhanced thixotropic properties and stability to adhesive products. Both organic and inorganic rheology modifying adhesives play a crucial role in adjusting the flow characteristics and performance of adhesives for various applications.</p></p>
