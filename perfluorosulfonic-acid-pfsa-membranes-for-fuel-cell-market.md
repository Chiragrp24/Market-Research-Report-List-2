<p><p>Perfluorosulfonic Acid (PFSA) membranes are widely used in fuel cell applications due to their high proton conductivity and chemical stability. They are categorized into three types based on the operating temperature range: low temperature (below 100°C), medium temperature (100-180°C), and high temperature (above 180°C) membranes. Low-temperature membranes are commonly used in polymer electrolyte membrane fuel cells (PEMFC) due to their high power density and fast start-up capabilities. Medium-temperature membranes offer improved efficiency and tolerance to impurities, making them suitable for direct methanol fuel cells (DMFC). Lastly, high-temperature membranes are utilized in solid oxide fuel cells (SOFC) due to their thermal and chemical stability under extreme conditions.</p></p>
