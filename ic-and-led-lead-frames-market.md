<p><p>IC and LED lead frames refer to the metal structures used to connect the integrated circuits (ICs) and light-emitting diodes (LEDs) to a printed circuit board (PCB). Different market types exist, including DIP (Dual In-line Package) with two parallel rows of pins, SOP (Small Outline Package) with smaller footprint and gull-wing leads, SOT (Small Outline Transistor) with three or more leads, QFP (Quad Flat Package) with leads on all four sides, DFN (Dual Flat No-Lead) with no pins extending from the package, QFN (Quad Flat No-Lead) with exposed pads on the underside, FC (Flat Circular) with circular shape, TO (Transistor Outline) with leads extending from a metal can, and others.</p></p>
